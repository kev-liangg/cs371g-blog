Hi there, I'm Kevin Liang, and this is my first blog entry for Spring 2021's CS373: Software Engineering.

This is my second course with Dr. Downing, and I'm sure you can visualize the passage of time with my portraits for each blog!

Where did you grow up?

I grew up in a suburban area near Salt Lake City, Utah. After middle school, my family moved to Scottsdale, Arizona for reasons that I forget now. I attended Desert Mountain High School and graduated under the International Baccalaureate Programme there.

What was your favorite extracurricular activity in high school?

Besides my school’s FRC robotics team, which I already talked about in previous blog posts, my other favorite extracurricular was Jazz Band. I played the Double Bass in an orchestra as well, but my best experiences came from performing jazz. I loved the combination of keeping time and improvising, as well as the adrenaline rush of taking solos.

I became very close with the other people in the band, as we stuck together through thick and thin; last-minute rehearsals, cramped bus rides, and the gratifying applause after our best performances. Yet despite all the good memories, we still inevitably drifted apart after high school. Sometimes I wonder if they still remember me, or at least the happier times we went through together.

Why did you come to UT?

In all honesty, I came to UT because I had inted the majority of my college applications and got rejected from all my top schools. I don’t like to think about what I could have done differently in hindsight, but I feel I’m perfectly happy being here at UT. Even if I did have to pay out-of-state tuition at first, I eventually was able to gain Texas residency as I realized that Austin was a great place to be as a Computer Science graduate.

Why are you in this class?

I signed up for this class because I heard great things from other students in Dr. Downing’s Generic Programming course over last summer. I really enjoy Dr. Downing’s lecture style as well as the independent project-focused nature of his courses, so registering as quickly as I can for this course was a no-brainer.

How much Javascript/Python/SQL/Web programming do you already know?

I am only comfortable with Python. I’ve been exposed to Javascript/SQL/Web programming minimally through some aspects of projects. I hope I can pick them up somewhat quickly before the pace of this course starts ramping up.

What are your expectations of this class?

My expectations of this course certainly changed with the first two lectures. I was expecting a similar format compared to Generic Programming, with the two-stage quizzes and exams. I suppose having no exams this semester will reduce the stress there. I’m glad the cold calling system stayed the same, as I feel it made all the lectures much more engaging and effective.

But completely honestly, I feel really bad about the quizzes this semester. Even with the previous two-stage format, they were my highest source of anxiety over the summer. I would wake up in the morning dry heaving, and I needed to skip breakfast on lecture days because I would physically vomit from the stress. I will definitely have to better prepare myself both mentally and physically to get through the quizzes as they are structured now.

What made you happy this week?

I finally drove back to my studio in Austin after spending the break with my father and his girlfriend, since I tested negative for covid. I’m just happy because I'll finally be able to interact with them once in a while, even if it means that I'll be completely alone for the majority of the time living here.
