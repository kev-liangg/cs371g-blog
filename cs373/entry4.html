Entry #4

  • February 14, 2021
  • Kevin Liang
What did you do this past week?

Last week, I finished the Collatz project and read the Pair Programming paper. As expected, I didn’t run into any difficulties with the assignment and everything went very smoothly.

What's in your way?

There isn’t anything in my way. I’ve been keeping up with the material in this course as well as looking over the project guidelines moving forward. I’m a bit concerned about the team building process since it will be random though, but I’m confident things will work out if I put the effort into it.

What will you do next week?

I’m not sure what I will do next week in light of campus closing down and even online courses being cancelled. I feel like this unforeseen event gave me a bit of breathing room though, so I’ll definitely be sure to step back and reassess if anything changes with my normal workflow.

If you read it, what did you think of the Pair Programming?

I thought the Pair Programming paper was really insightful, as well as reflective of how programming principles used to be different. From my point of view, it seems really obvious that working in pairs is the most effective way to deliver code, but apparently that didn’t always use to be the reigning attitude. I would personally appreciate it a lot more if more classes gave us the opportunity to use pair programming on the assignments, but I guess we can’t have everyone putting out the best quality assignments apparently.

What was your experience of Collatz?

I really like the Collatz project and think it’s perfect to start out this class. It applies a lot of what we’re learning in lecture and reading in Perusall in terms of software development best practices, and leaves a lot of independent inquiry into concepts like caching and optimizations.

What was your experience of types?

I mostly was able to follow the idea of typing, though it was definitely on the stranger end as I’m more used to statically typed languages versus whatever Python has going on. Either way I think the Python implementation is interesting to say the least, though I realize I still need to go through the Typescript equivalent and see if it’s more similar or different in that regard.

What made you happy this week?

I turned 21 on Friday! I wasted no time in buying some hard liquor with my newfound privileges. Having access to actual alcohol this year really came in handy to numb the pain of being all alone on yet another Valentine’s day.

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

My tip-of-the-week is GitHub Student Developer Pack. My tip-of-the-week is the GitHub Student Developer Pack. As we’re all students here, GitHub provides us with a lot of free deals and offers on a lot of industry standard technologies for our educational use. Whether it’s for use in this class or kickstarting a personal project, there’s plenty of opportunity to take advantage of the offers there and build experience for the future.
