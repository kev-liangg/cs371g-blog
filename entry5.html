Entry #5

  • July 5, 2020
  • Kevin Liang
What did you do this past week?

For the first half of this past week, I studied for the first exam. After the test, I looked over the material for the Integer project and started working on it with Kevin Gao. We quickly progressed through all the operations and negative cases aside from multiplication and power, which we still need to implement. I also finished annotating the provided code on the Vector data structure. Although I am quite familiar with it from my previous C++ experience, I was still uncertain about a few things.

What's in your way?

We still need to implement the multiplication and power operations using the Karatsuba algorithm and ensure we pass all the HackerRank test cases, especially the last one that seems to greatly stress the program runtime and optimization. I have looked into other sources of performance improvement from the Java BigInteger documentation, which we may have to also learn if the Karatsuba algorithm doesn't provide the necessary speed.

What will you do next week?

Next week, Kevin and I will focus our efforts on optimizing our multiplication and power operations to pass the HackerRank tests. We hope we will be able to implement everything without much issue, though there is still the possibility that further optimizations will be needed. In that case, we will have to do additional work to account for those as well.

What was your experience of Test #1 and its two-stage nature?

I felt very prepared for Test #1 and didn't have any issues with the two-stage nature. At first, I heard some negative things about how the two parts meant there was less time to answer the questions. However, I didn't feel rushed at all as I finished both stages with plenty of time to spare. Furthermore, the second collaborative part gave me a lot more confidence in my answers which made me feel a lot better than usual after a test.

How are you doing and holding up? What's been most helpful for you in terms of support at this time?

I am feeling well, now that the first exam has passed. The downtime with no class on Friday really helped me the most, as I was able to make up for the hours of sleep sacrificed to study again before the exam. It was a great mental reset before shifting gears into the Integer project, which I have enjoyed working on so far.

What made you happy this week?

It is quite superficial, but my happiest point of this week was again related to the card game Hearthstone. I finally was able to afford one of my favorite decks (Highlander Priest, for those of you who know it), which has brought me a good amount of enjoyment in playing the game.

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

While working on the Integer project, we ran into a very nasty bug pertaining to vectors and iterators. We tried to do a push_back operation to grow the vector before iterating over it, but we got completely wrong values from the iterator despite the vector containing the correct values and everything else successfully running. This ended up being caused by iterator invalidation, which didn't seem to actually be detected at all by the compiler or cppcheck.

I found this article outlining the specific reasons behind the behavior, which all makes sense in hindsight. Since push_back grew the vector itself, the correct values must have been copied to a larger block of memory making the old iterators return bizarre numbers. Hence, we learned to always redeclare iterators to be safe, especially when working with non-const vectors.
