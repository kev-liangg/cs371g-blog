Entry #6

  • October 3, 2021
  • Kevin Liang
What did you do this past week?

This past week, I successfully caught up on all my project deadlines in my other courses. It took me a bit longer than I thought since I wasn't at my usual work environment for most of the week, but I still managed to get things done.

What's in your way?

I still have to finish the unit tests and workflow tasks for the Voting project, but that should only take me a few hours at the most. I also have an upcoming deliverable for one of my other courses that requires group work, so most of my time will likely be spent there working as a team. That is always more complicated as it's reliant on our overall availability, which has been a fairly major blocker so far.

What will you do next week?

I will finish up the Voting tasks due Monday, and then spend most of the week on my other course deliverable. I also need to finish up my recruiting processes, as that October 15 deadline is rapidly approaching.

If you read it, what did you think of the Single Responsibility Principle?

I felt there was room for improvement in the paper. Although the overall idea was concise and not too complicated, the choice of wording in some of the phrases seemed to be pretty arbitrary. I think a better way to convey the ideas would be to use more code and design examples, and even possibly walking through a design process by starting with a set of requirements and designing classes from there, rather than starting with the rectangle example. Also, a lot of the code compilation/deployment points seemed to be glossed over, to the detriment of someone who knows less about compilers and cloud deployment.

What was your experience of stack, queue, priority_queue, values, pointers, and references?

I had first learned most of these concepts in Dr. Downing's Generic Programming course, so the material this week was a good refresher in terms of backing containers and more specific behavior.

What made you happy this week?

Another Beast In Black single dropped! The release of the full album can't come soon enough.

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

My tip-of-the-week is to make a habit of using GitLab's CI Lint tool. It's easy to make silly mistakes in the yaml formatting, so taking a bit of time to plug it into the linter can save some red in your pipeline.
