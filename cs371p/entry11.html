Entry #11

  • November 7, 2021
  • Kevin Liang
What did you do this past week?

This past week, I got started on the Darwin assignment with my partner. We were able to set up our environment for pair programming and quickly put together a naive solution to the HackerRank problem and integrate the input and output formats. Other than that, I met a major group project deliverable for my iOS mobile programming course so it's good to have that out of the way.

What's in your way?

We still need to implement more comprehensive unit tests for our project after debugging the correctness issues surrounding the HackerRank submission. We also will need to refactor our design to be more consistent with the Object-Oriented Principles and delegation patterns we're reading about in the papers and reflect that in our UML design.

What will you do next week?

I will finish up the project refactoring and finalize our design and unit tests with my partner to be more comfortable within the deadline, as we have correctness out of the way.

If you read it, what did you think of the Paper #11: Getters and Setters?

I've read the paper before in Software Engineering, but I really didn't understand or retain the knowledge as it was so unrelated from both the lectures and what we were doing as our project. Reading it again in this course while needing to do an actual Object-Oriented assignment was definitely more appropriate. That said, I still feel that it's really late in the semester to finally get to the OO part of OOP in this class. I've already had a much better lecture and understanding of the delegate pattern through my iOS mobile computing course early on, and would more likely attribute any knowledge gained from the paper to that course.

What was your experience of vector?

I've delved into the STL implementation of vector before in Dr. Downing's Generic Programming course, so everything this week was simple and familiar.

What made you happy this week?

I've reevaluated my life priorities and am happy to say that I'm back on bulking season. Actively bouldering has certainly improved my appetite over the past month, so I think I finally have enough mental motivation to start eating big and finally fueling some hard gains again.

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

I read a recent psychological science study that linked listening to music before bedtime to worsened sleep quality. I know a lot of people, including myself, who enjoy listening to music while programming whether for increased productivity, help focusing, or simply as an enjoyable multitask. But regardless of our motivation, it may be helpful to evaluate our listening habits in the evenings if we still find ourselves working after hours, lest our sleep be negatively affected.
