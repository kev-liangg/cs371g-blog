Entry #10

  • October 31, 2021
  • Kevin Liang
What did you do this past week?

I didn't have to do much this past week, seeing as it was mostly our break week after the project deadline. I finished up a quick and easy assignment in Symbolic Programming, and then got started on some group project work in my iOS mobile computing course, as I have an upcoming deadline and it's always good to get started earlier on those things. Other than that, I took the time to mentally relax a bit, not being afraid to squander some time on video games, socializing, sleeping in, and bouldering.

What's in your way?

I only have the project milestone in iOS mobile computing to complete and turn in as a group. The work shouldn't be too involved or difficult seeing as we have four people to delegate everything, as well as a set of clearly defined deliverables that we will have to figure out. We worked until the deadline on the last milestone, so hopefully we can get everything packaged up with a bit more leeway this next week. I also still have to turn in the next Symbolic Programming assignment, which the professor said should be fairly simple and fun given that we only have a week for this one.

What will you do next week?

Besides meeting those assignment deadlines, I will probably spend more time bouldering. I'm definitely feeling a lot of progression from where I started, and I don't want to get lazy.

If you read it, what did you think of the Paper #10: Dependency Inversion Principle?

I liked how the paper further built up on the principles so far, reiterating the idea of clients/delegates, single responsibility, and overall abstraction. The point about abstractions coming before the implementation was really important, as we're used to intuitively thinking about the details first. I just wish we had more opportunities to apply these principles through our coursework. We've been learning about C++ syntax and behavior for so long now, and applying designs in light of OOP would certainly reinforce the learning within the papers.

What was your experience of iteration, functions, and heap arrays?

All of these concepts were intuitive and familiar to me, as I've used them all more or less in practice. It was definitely interesting to compare and contrast how functions and callables could be used in C++ with a functional language like Lisp/Clojure that I'm learning in Symbolic Programming.

What made you happy this week?
What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

My tip-of-the-week is to listen to your body, especially to avoid tunnel vision when we're in the thick of a codebase. Know the signs of dehydration headaches, and fix those with not just water but also electrolytes if needed. Don't ignore hunger pangs, it's never worth trying to grind away at something because that empty stomach is already hindering your productivity. Take a quick break and eat something and get back on track. Be mindful of your posture if you feel aches or pains, especially if you've been sitting too long working on an assignment. Walk around for a bit and stretch, or even get some fresh air. We only get one body so we need to take care of it as long as we're alive.
