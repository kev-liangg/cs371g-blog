Entry #5

  • September 26, 2021
  • Kevin Liang
What did you do this past week?

This past week, I implemented and finished the HackerRank submission for Project 2: Australian Voting. It was fairly simple overall in terms of the object-oriented design I chose and the optimizations, though I did run into a few obscure bugs that took a bit longer to resolve. Interestingly, one of those bugs I ran into just so "worked" with the HackerRank input without fixing it if the sort was unstable, but went wrong with a stable sort. I'm glad I was able to catch that by chance though, as it's likely I would have had to rediscover the bug through other acceptance tests even though I'd pass 3/3 HackerRank test cases.

What's in your way?

I need to complete the remaining workflow tasks for the project as well as finalize my unit tests now that the correctness of my program has been confirmed.

What will you do next week?

Unfortunately, I will have to prioritize other homework and project deadlines over the next week in my other courses and hence set aside the remaining tasks for Project 2. I'm a bit behind again in my other courses, plus I need to do some real-life tasks which cuts into a lot of the time I have.

If you read it, what did you think of the Paper #5: Pair Programming?

I liked the paper overall, and the concept definitely holds up today despite its more dated publication. Unfortunately, I feel there's less incentive to work with a partner with the new grading scheme as a virtual token is much less of a motivator than extra credit. But I've had very positive experiences with pair programming in previous iterations of Dr. Downing's courses, so it's definitely something I'd be willing to accept for more difficult future projects.

What was your experience of vector, list, and deque, and invalidation?

I was already fully familiar with all of these concepts, as I remember them from my Generic Programming course I also took with Dr. Downing two summers ago.

What made you happy this week?

Not exactly this week, but my new Tesla is finally being delivered on Monday!

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

HackerRank purposely hides the output of the project test cases, which can be particularly frustrating when it keeps spitting out "Wrong Answer" with no other details. What helped me debug a bit more was to use the fact it has a separate debug message for "Abort Called" which can be triggered with a purposely failing assertion. Hence, to determine the nature of the test cases, I used certain conditionals with an assert(false) to see if the test case satisfied those conditions, which would be exposed by that "Abort Called" message instead of "Wrong Answer."
