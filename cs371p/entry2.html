Entry #2

  • September 5, 2021
  • Kevin Liang
What did you do this past week?

I figured out how to pass all of the HackerRank test cases for our first Collatz project, as well as finished reading and annotating the Makefile paper. I actually worked a bit backwards for this project by typing out my work in-line with the HackerRank tool from the blank template, in order to simulate the environment of an actual online assessment.

What's in your way?

The main thing I have to do is transfer my HackerRank submission into the expected file templates in the public code repo that Dr. Downing provided us, and then follow the workflow expectations for the rest of the project. I also still need to complete the academic integrity quiz.

What will you do next week?

Next week, I will plan on finishing the majority of the Collatz project as well as submit the academic integrity quiz. I also have some homeworks due for my other two courses that seem a bit programming-heavy, so I will likely start on that on Monday's day off.

If you read it, what did you think of the Paper #2: Makefile

I've been able to get a lot more actual experience with Makefiles since last semester, so I felt much more comfortable reading and annotating this paper. There were still some very interesting components, like how Dr. Downing wrote a generic rule to run a test that gets invoked using the naming convention of the student test files in the public test repo.

What was your experience of assertions, unit tests, and coverage?

I understood all of these topics, as they were conveyed clearly in the lectures. I actually had the opportunity to talk specifics about assertions and unit tests in a technical interview right after Friday's lecture, and it definitely seemed to help with the conversation.

What made you happy this week?

One of my favorite bands, Beast In Black, released a single out of their new album coming later this year. I would've never expected a cheesy music video out of Cyberpunk and Blade Runner and teen romance novels, but you simply can't argue with their magnificent 80's heavy sound.

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?
Stay hydrated! Speaking from much experience, it's all too easy to forget to drink water while grinding away at school and other projects. There are plenty of apps to help with reminders, such as this native Chrome Extension. Additionally, keep in mind that only drinking water without eating doesn't work either as the body needs the electrolytes we obtain from foods. So don't forget to eat enough either, and maybe keep some Pedialyte on hand to fix the inevitable dehydration because we're all imperfect.
