Entry #6

  • July 12, 2020
  • Kevin Liang
What did you do this past week?

This past week, I completed the Integer project and started working on the Deque implementation due next week. I also finished reading and annotating the assigned article about "The Joel Test," a humorous and well-written basic checklist for better code development. Although it was originally written two decades ago, all of the material still seems very relevant for any software company; certainly, there are still companies out there that would not pass the test at all.

What's in your way?

My teammate and I still have a lot of work left on the Deque assignment. We have a good understanding of the higher-level concept of the Deque the iterator behaviors, but we still have not figured out how to use the allocators to actually store the data.

What will you do next week?

Next week, my partner and I will likely look through the implementation of Vector closely in order to adapt the code to work with elements in a Deque. Hopefully that shouldn't take long to figure out, which means we can correctly implement more of the intended behavior of our Deque.

What was your experience of Vector and Deque nature?

I have a solid conceptual understanding of both the Vector and Deque, including both of their use cases and the practical differences between them. However, the current project of implementing the Deque still challenges my comprehension. In the end, I know I will have a far greater understanding of both concepts after meticulously parsing through the given implementation of Vector and constructing the actual code behind a Deque.

How are you doing and holding up? What's been most helpful for you in terms of support at this time?

I've been feeling relatively insignificant at times, but other than that I'm not doing too badly. The last project required a lot of mental effort, and the current project seems even more difficult, so I expect that things will go downhill from here. As for support, I've found it helpful to greatly increase the intensity of my workouts. I have been forcing myself to add more repetitions with minimal rest periods in order to take advantage of those negative emotions.

What made you happy this week?

I started watching an older TV comedy series with a couple of my friends. I really enjoy the humor and the characters, and I am happy we have been able to share many laughs together that way.

What's your pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

Sometimes writing good code isn't all about style. In the case of C++, there are many destructive behaviors that compilers cannot detect, with some more obvious than others. To help with that, a company called Perforce developed the High Integrity C++ (HIC++) coding standard.

This standard is a list of best practices in order to write high-quality and safe C++. There are other popular standards as well, with a lot of the rules overlapping between them. For example, JSF AV C++ is another coding standard from the development of the F35 Joint Strike Fighter. Although this class has slightly lower stakes than a 5th-generation stealth fighter jet, it might still be a good idea to refer to these standards to produce the safest code.
